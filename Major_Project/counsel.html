Psychological Care After Stroke

When stroke patients do not receive adequate support for mental health, it can hinder recovery and reduce motivation to pursue rehabilitation.

By treating stroke patients holistically – by taking into account mental and social factors, rather than just the physical symptoms of stroke – a better outcome can be achieved.

You’re about to learn why psychological care is important for stroke patients and how you can approach it.



The Virtuous Cycle of Psychological Care

When stroke patients can receive psychological care during the early stages of recovery, it can help improve levels of confidence, motivation, and wellbeing.

The benefits of psychological care create a virtuous cycle for the stroke patient. Unlike a vicious cycle, where negative events reinforce themselves, a virtuous cycle occurs when positive events reinforce themselves.

More psychological care results in more positive mood and confidence. Positive mood and confidence motivate more action towards recovery, which results in more physical improvements.



Improving Psychological Care After Stroke

The aftermath of a stroke can take a devastating toll on patients’ psychological health.

To negate the effects of post-stroke depression and other psychological disorders, better psychological care is needed.

Experiment with different modalities like talk therapy or positive psychology to help improve your confidence and mood.

This can result in a virtuous cycle where your recovery continues to improve.

The Need for Psychological Care After Stroke

A stroke is a life-threatening condition that often leaves significant aftermath, including impaired mobility, impaired speech and/or cognition, difficulty eating, and inability to use the bathroom independently.

Between 35%-60% of stroke patients experience cognitive impairments as well.

More than a third of stroke survivors struggle with post-stroke depression, the most common psychiatric disorder after stroke.

With the majority of stroke patients struggling with new cognitive, physical, and emotional distress, psychological care should be prioritized.



Types of Psychological Care After Stroke

If you would like to seek psychological care for yourself or a loved one, talk to your doctor or medical team. Your doctor may refer you to a psychiatrist or other health specialist.

Methods for psychological care after stroke include psychotherapy, positive psychology, mindfulness, and cognitive behavior therapy (CBT).

These modalities can help with a variety of psychiatric disorders including post-stroke depression and can improve psychological wellbeing.

Experiment with different types of psychological treatments until you find one that’s right for you.



Seeking Psychological Care

If you’re interested in a holistic approach to recovery, consider the various types of psychological care available.

Discuss these options with your medical team to find the best approach for your individual needs.
